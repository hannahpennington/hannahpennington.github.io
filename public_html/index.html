Education

I am currently in the Medical Scientist Training Program at the University of Illinois at Chicago, earning my MD/PhD. I have completed the pre-clinical portion of medical school, and am now in the Merrill Lab in the Department of Biochemistry and Molecular Genetics, studying DNA Repair using CRISPR-Cas9. I also serve as Vice President of Student Life for the Chicago Medical Student Council (our Student Government at the Medical School), and am a peer education tutor for the College of Medicine.

In the future I plan to work in Pediatrics, either specializing in Hematology/Oncology or Neonatology, using gene therapy to improve clinical outcomes.

Originally from Kentucky, I studied at the Gatton Academy of Mathematics and Science. After completing the curriculum with distinction, I completed my BS at Western Kentucky University, studying Biology, Chemistry, and Mathematics. During my time at Gatton and WKU, I worked with Richard Schugart, modeling the interactions of MMP-1, TIMPs, and TGF-Beta in the wound healing environment. My thesis work studied the application of Latin Hypercube Sampling and Partial Rank Correlation Coefficient Analysis in mathematical models of wound healing, including those simulating oxygen therapy, bacterial infection, and the normal healing process.
